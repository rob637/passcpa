Audit workpaper documentation... Get this wrong, and you're GONE on the CISA exam.

Audit workpapers are the bedrock of any IS audit. Think of them as the complete record of your audit... documenting the procedures performed, evidence obtained, and conclusions reached. Auditors care deeply about thorough documentation because it provides support for the audit opinion, allows for review and replication, and ensures accountability. Without solid workpapers, your audit is just... your word.

ISACA loves to trip you up on the scope of what *must* be documented. The trap is focusing solely on *findings*. Sure, findings are crucial... but the correct answer is always the *broadest* option: all significant matters, including the planning, scope, procedures, evidence, and conclusions. Don't just document the problems; document *everything* relevant to the audit. Always choose the most comprehensive answer.

That complete picture is what distinguishes a strong auditor.

Remember this: comprehensive workpaper documentation is your shield against challenge. Master it, and you'll conquer the CISA exam.
