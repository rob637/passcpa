Not all software is built in-house. Understanding the acquisition process and vendor management helps auditors assess risks in purchased solutions. <break time='1s'/> The acquisition process typically follows stages. <break time='0.8s'/> Requirements definition: What do we need? Before looking at products, document the detailed requirements. Functional requirements describe what it must do. Non-functional requirements describe performance, security, scalability. <break time='0.8s'/> Market research: What's available? Understand the vendor landscape. Identify potential products. Sometimes there's no suitable product, and you build custom. <break time='0.8s'/> Request for proposal, or R.F.P.: Formally solicit vendor responses. The R.F.P. describes requirements, evaluation criteria, timeline, and contractual requirements. Vendors respond with proposals describing their solution, pricing, and qualifications. <break time='0.8s'/> Evaluation: Score proposals against defined criteria. Criteria might include functionality, cost, vendor stability, implementation support, references. Conduct demonstrations and reference checks. <break time='0.8s'/> Contract negotiation: Define terms of the agreement. Include service levels, support obligations, pricing, intellectual property rights, and termination provisions. <break time='0.8s'/> Implementation: Configure, integrate, test, and deploy the solution. <break time='1s'/> Vendor management extends beyond acquisition. Ongoing oversight includes: <break time='0.5s'/> Performance monitoring: Is the vendor meeting service levels? <break time='0.5s'/> Relationship management: Regular communication and issue resolution. <break time='0.5s'/> Risk management: Monitoring vendor financial health, security practices, and compliance. <break time='0.5s'/> Contract management: Tracking obligations, renewals, and changes. <break time='1s'/> Audit focus: Was a documented evaluation process followed? Do contracts protect organizational interests? Is vendor performance monitored? Are risks appropriately managed?
