You've been hired as the new IT Audit Director for a mid-sized financial services company. The previous director retired, and the IT audit function has been inactive for eighteen months. The board wants a capable IT audit function within six months. Where do you start? <break time='1s'/> You begin with discovery. <break time='0.5s'/> You meet with key stakeholders: the Chief Audit Executive, the C.I.O., the C.I.S.O., business unit leaders, and the audit committee chair. You want to understand their concerns, their priorities, and their expectations. <break time='0.5s'/> You review existing documentation: previous audit reports, organizational charts, IT strategic plans, risk assessments, regulatory examination findings. <break time='0.5s'/> You assess the current state of IT. What systems exist? What changes are planned? What incidents have occurred? <break time='1s'/> Next, you develop a risk assessment. <break time='0.5s'/> You identify the IT universe: all systems, applications, processes, and projects that could be audited. <break time='0.5s'/> You assess risk for each area considering inherent risk factors and control environment. Regulatory sensitive areas get higher risk ratings. Systems with recent incidents get higher ratings. Areas never audited or not audited recently get higher ratings. <break time='0.5s'/> You prioritize based on risk. Limited resources mean you can't audit everything. Focus on the highest risks first. <break time='1s'/> You develop the audit plan. <break time='0.5s'/> Based on resources and risk assessment, you determine which audits to conduct in the next year. You consider regulatory requirements, management concerns, and elapsed time since last audit. <break time='0.5s'/> You present the plan to the audit committee for approval. <break time='1s'/> You establish infrastructure. <breaktml='0.5s'/> You create standard methodologies: risk assessment approach, work paper standards, reporting templates. <break time='0.5s'/> You determine staffing needs and begin recruiting or developing the team. <break time='0.5s'/> You establish relationships with co-source partners if internal resources aren't sufficient. <break time='1s'/> You begin executing audits. <break time='0.5s'/> Start with one or two audits to work out the process. Apply lessons learned to subsequent audits. Build credibility through professional, value-added work. <break time='0.5s'/> Within six months, you have a functioning IT audit group with a risk-based plan, executing quality audits.
