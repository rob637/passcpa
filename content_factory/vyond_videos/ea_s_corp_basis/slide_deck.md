### Narration Script (35 seconds)
> "What happens when allocated losses exceed total basis? First, losses reduce stock basis. If stock basis hits zero, then - and ONLY then - losses can reduce debt basis. How do you get more basis? Additional capital contributions, additional direct loans, or future income allocations. The exam loves to test this: if a shareholder has $30,000 allocated losses but only $20,000 total basis, they deduct $20,000 this year and carry forward $10,000."

### Narration Script (30 seconds)
> "For S corporations with accumulated earnings and profits - usually from prior C corporation years - distributions get more complex. The Accumulated Adjustments Account, or AAA, tracks earnings accumulated since the S election. Distributions come first from AAA - these are tax-free to the extent of stock basis, just like regular S corp distributions. If AAA is exhausted, then distributions come from E&P - these are taxed as dividends. After E&P, it's return of basis, then capital gain. If the S corp was NEVER a C corp, there's no E&P, and you skip the dividend layer. Much simpler."
