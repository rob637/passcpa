## Positioning shift — why we downplay "AI"

- "AI-powered" reads as low-credibility to working professionals studying for a $2,500–$3,500 license exam.
- Our actual moat is the **9,150+ expert-written MCQ bank**, the **why-wrong** explanations, and the **pass guarantee**.
- Lead with content depth and honest pricing. Mention adaptive/spaced repetition as features — never as the headline promise.

## Campaign architecture

Built in [src/services/growth/campaignBlueprints.ts](../src/services/growth/campaignBlueprints.ts).

### Campaign A — CPA Exam Prep (5 ad groups)
| Ad group | Theme | Max CPC | Why it exists |
|---|---|---|---|
| Generic | High-volume head terms | $1.40 | Catches "cpa exam prep", "cpa review course" |
| Practice Questions | Volume + intent | $1.55 | Highest converting category for a question bank |
| Question Quality | High-intent long-tail | $1.65 | Buyers who already know they need explanations |
| Competitor Alternatives | Conquest | $1.80 | "becker alternative", "cheaper than becker" — these convert |
| Section Specific | Per-section intent | $1.20 | FAR/AUD/REG/BAR/ISC/TCP study guide queries |

### Campaign B — CPA Daily Questions (3 ad groups)
| Ad group | Theme | Max CPC | Why it exists |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standalone | Direct intent | $1.20 | "cpa question of the day", "daily cpa question" — bullseye |
| Becker Supplement | Cross-sell | $1.40 | Working pros doing Becker want extra reps |
| Study Habit | Long-tail | $0.95 | "how to study cpa daily" — cheaper, builds remarketing pool |

All copy fits Google's hard limits: ≤30 chars headline, ≤90 chars description, ≤25 chars sitelink text, ≤35 chars sitelink description. Validated automatically by the exporter (run prints "Validation issues: 0").

## What the user (you) needs to do

I've done the engineering: blueprints, exporter, validation, generated CSV. The remaining steps are the irreducibly-human ones — Google Ads UI work plus the launch decision.

### Step 1 — Grab the CSV (already generated)
File path:
```
scripts/sem/output/voraprep-flagship-2026-04-28.csv
```
If you want to regenerate (e.g., new budgets):
```bash
node scripts/sem/export-google-ads-csv.cjs                     # defaults
node scripts/sem/export-google-ads-csv.cjs --cpa-budget 50 --daily-budget 30
```

### Step 2 — Import into Google Ads Editor (10 min)
1. Open **Google Ads Editor** (free desktop app: https://ads.google.com/intl/en/home/tools/ads-editor/).
2. Pick the VoraPrep account → **Account → Import → From file**.
3. Select `voraprep-flagship-2026-04-28.csv`.
4. Click **Process file** → review the diff (you should see 2 campaigns, 8 ad groups, 78 keywords, 8 RSAs).
5. Click **Post**.

Everything lands as **Paused**. Nothing is spending yet.

### Step 3 — Wire conversion tracking in Google Ads (15 min, one-time)
Create three conversion actions in **Tools → Conversions → New conversion action → Website**:

| Conversion | Category | Value | Count | Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `daily_signup` | Sign-up | $5 (LTV-discounted) | One | Stripe Checkout success for `cpa-daily` (event you already fire) |
| `cpa_trial_start` | Sign-up | $20 | One | Trial start on `/cpa` (existing event) |
| `cpa_subscription` | Purchase | Use transaction value | One | Stripe webhook `customer.subscription.created` for CPA Prep |

You already emit these to GA4 via `src/services/analytics.ts`. Easiest path:
- Link GA4 ↔ Google Ads (**Tools → Linked accounts → GA4**).
- In Google Ads, **import** these as conversions from GA4 (don't re-instrument).

### Step 4 — Verify UTM landing (2 min)
Open one of the ad URLs from the CSV in your browser. Confirm:
- Page loads `https://voraprep.com/cpa?utm_source=google&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=cpa-prep&...`
- The signup/checkout button preserves `utm_*` into Firestore (check `users/{uid}.acquisition.utm`).

### Step 5 — Flip the first ad group to Enabled (the launch decision)
**Recommended first ad group: `Daily CPA - Standalone`.**

Why: lowest target CPA ($6), cleanest intent ("cpa question of the day"), $4.99 entry price = lowest psychological barrier. Best signal/dollar to validate the funnel.

In Google Ads UI:
1. Open the **CPA Daily Questions — SMS Drills (2026)** campaign.
2. Set campaign status → **Enabled**.
3. Open **Daily CPA - Standalone** ad group → **Enabled**.
4. Leave the other 7 ad groups Paused.

### Step 6 — Day-1 sanity check (within 4 hours)
- Search Terms report: ensure the queries triggering us look on-intent. Add irrelevant ones to negatives.
- Check first impressions and CTR. RSA "Combinations" view shows which headlines pair.
- Confirm at least one click landed on `/daily-cpa` with UTMs intact (GA4 Realtime).

### Step 7 — 48-hour review
- Standalone ad group should have ~5–15 clicks at $1.00–$1.40 avg CPC.
- If CTR > 4% and Search Lost IS (rank) is high, raise max CPC by 20%.
- If a single query is eating budget without converting, add it as Negative Exact.
- Once you have **3+ paid conversions** in Standalone, enable **Becker Supplement**.

### Step 8 — Week-1 review
- Move winning headlines to position 1 (pin in RSA editor).
- Enable **CPA Exam Prep → Practice Questions** ad group (highest-converting category for a question bank).
- Review Quality Score per keyword. Anything below 5 → rewrite landing copy or pause.

## What we deliberately did NOT do

- **No Performance Max / Display / YouTube** — Search only for v1. PMax can't be properly negativized at this scale and would burn budget on irrelevant placements.
- **No "AI tutor" headlines** — saved for content marketing, not paid Search.
- **No money-back-guarantee claims** — our guarantee is "study free until you pass". Misrepresenting it would tank trust.
- **No competitor brand bidding** beyond `<brand> alternative` — bidding on raw "becker" is policy-risky and the alternative-modifier captures the real intent.
- **No automated bidding (tCPA / Max Conversions)** until we have ≥30 conversions. Manual CPC for the first 2 weeks of learning data.
