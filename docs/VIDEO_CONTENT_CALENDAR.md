# CPA Exam Video Content Calendar

This document outlines high-impact, notorious topics for short-form video content (TikTok/Shorts/Reels). Each topic is chosen for its difficulty and "pain point" status among students.

## 🟢 **FAR (Financial Accounting & Reporting)** — *The Beast*

| Topic | The Hook | Key Concept / Mnemonic | Visual Idea |
|-------|----------|------------------------|-------------|
| **1. Consolidated Financial Statements (CAR IN BIG)** | "Why do 40% of students fail FAR? Consolidation adjustments." | **CAR IN BIG**: **C**ommon stock, **A**PIC, **R**etained earnings (eliminate sub's equity), **I**nvestment in sub (eliminate parent's asset), **N**on-controlling interest (create), **B**alance sheet adj to fair value, **I**ntangible assets, **G**oodwill (plug). | Visual of a "Big Parent" eating a "Small Sub." Kinetic text of "CAR IN BIG" slamming onto screen as pieces disappear. |
| **2. Leases (Lessee Accounting)** | "Is it Finance or Operating? Get this wrong, fail the SIM." | **OWNES**: **O**wnership transfer, **W**ritten option to purchase, **N**et present value (90%), **E**conomic life (75%), **S**pecialized asset. If ANY are met = Finance. | Split screen: "Finance Lease" (fancy car) vs "Operating Lease" (rental car). Checkboxes appearing next to O-W-N-E-S letters. |
| **3. Bonds (Effective Interest Method)** | "Stop memorizing the amortization table. Draw the T-Account." | **Interest Expense vs. Cash Paid**: Expense = Carrying Value × Market Rate. Cash = Face Value × Coupon Rate. Plug goes to Amortization. | Animated T-Account. "Expense" bucket fills up based on market rate. "Cash" bucket leaks out based on coupon. Difference = Amortization. |
| **4. Government Accounting (Fund Types)** | "Government accounting isn't hard, it's just weird. Meet the GRaSPP." | **GRaSPP SE PAPI**: **G**eneral, **R**evenue (Special), **a**nd **S**ervice (Debt), **P**rojects (Capital), **P**ermanent. **S**ervice (Internal), **E**nterprise. **P**ension, **A**gency, **P**rivate Purpose, **I**nvestment (Custodial). | Visual of a "Government City." Different buildings for each fund type (City Hall = General, Water Plant = Enterprise). |
| **5. Statement of Cash Flows (Indirect Method)** | "The one SIM guaranteed to show up on your exam." | **CLAD**: **C**urrent assets/liabilities changes, **L**osses/Gains (add/subtract), **A**mortization/Depreciation (add back), **D**eferred items. | A literal "Cash Flow Waterfall." Start with Net Income at top. Add back Non-Cash Expenses (rocks falling in). Adjust for Working Capital (water flowing in/out). |

## 🟡 **AUD (Audit & Attestation)** — *The Tricky One*

| Topic | The Hook | Key Concept / Mnemonic | Visual Idea |
|-------|----------|------------------------|-------------|
| **1. Audit Opinions (The Hierarchy)** | "Unmodified vs. Unqualified vs. Adverse. Do you know the difference?" | **Materiality + Pervasiveness**: Not Material = Unmodified. Material but Not Pervasive = Qualified ("Except for"). Material AND Pervasive = Adverse (GAAP issue) or Disclaimer (Scope issue). | A "Traffic Light" system. Green = Unmodified. Yellow = Qualified. Red = Adverse/Disclaimer. Show a "Pervasive" fog covering the whole financial statement. |
| **2. Internal Control Components (CRIME)** | "The COSO Cube is boring. Remember CRIME." | **CRIME**: **C**ontrol Environment (Tone at top), **R**isk Assessment, **I**nformation & Communication, **M**onitoring, **E**xisting Control Activities. | Noir/Detective theme. "Solving a CRIME." Magnifying glass hovering over each letter. "Tone at the Top" showing the CEO. |
| **3. Transaction Cycles (Revenue/Expenditure)** | "Tracing vs. Vouching. Get this backwards and you score zero." | **Tracing** (Up/Forward): Source Doc → Journal → Ledger (Completeness). **Vouching** (Down/Backward): Ledger → Journal → Source Doc (Existence/Occurrence). | Visual of an elevator. "Going UP" = Tracing (building completeness). "Going DOWN" = Vouching (verifying existence). |
| **4. Assertions (COVER U)** | "The 6 things you must prove for every account balance." | **COVER U**: **C**ompleteness, cut-**O**ff, **V**aluation/allocation, **E**xistence/occurrence, **R**ights & obligations, **U**nderstandability/classification. | A shield with "COVER U" on it protecting the Financial Statements. Icons for each (e.g., calendar for Cut-off, price tag for Valuation). |
| **5. SSARS/SSAE (Preparation vs Compilation vs Review)** | "No Assurance vs Limited Assurance. What do you need?" | **Preparation** (No report, no assurance) vs **Compilation** (Report, no assurance) vs **Review** (Limited assurance, inquiry/analytics) vs **Audit** (Positive assurance). | A stepped pyramid. Base = Prep (easiest). Step 2 = Comp. Step 3 = Review (Analytics/Inquiry icons). Top = Audit (Evidence/Observation). |

## 🔴 **REG (Regulation)** — *The Rule Book*

| Topic | The Hook | Key Concept / Mnemonic | Visual Idea |
|-------|----------|------------------------|-------------|
| **1. Basis (S-Corp vs Partnership)** | "The #1 reason people fail REG: Basis calculations." | **Debt Basis**: S-Corps generally do NOT get debt basis (unless direct loan). Partnerships DO get debt basis (recourse vs nonrecourse). | "The Wall of Basis." Show S-Corp shareholder hitting a wall when trying to deduct losses > stock basis. Show Partner climbing over the wall using a "Debt Ladder." |
| **2. Mom & Pop Exception (Passive Losses)** | "Can you deduct rental losses? Usually no. Unless you're Mom & Pop." | **$25k Rule**: Can deduct up to $25k passive loss if active participant. Phase-out starts at $100k AGI, gone at $150k. | Visual of a lovely Mom & Pop rental house. A greedy "IRS Hand" tries to take the loss deduction away. A shield appears if AGI < $100k. |
| **3. Contracts (Statute of Frauds)** | "When does a contract HAVE to be in writing? Remember MY LEGS." | **MY LEGS**: **M**arriage, **Y**ear (can't perform within 1 year), **L**and, **E**xecutor, **G**oods > $500, **S**urety (Guarantor). | Animated legs walking. Text overlays on each step: "Marriage... Year... Land...". Stop sign at "Goods > $500". |
| **4. Individual Tax Formula (For AGI vs From AGI)** | "Above the Line vs Below the Line. It changes everything." | **For AGI** (Above): Business exp, student loan interest, IRA. **From AGI** (Below): Standard/Itemized (Medical, SALT, Interest, Charity). | Visual of a "Line" drawn across the screen. Items falling from the sky. Good items stick "Above" the line. Bad items fall "Below" (harder to deduct). |
| **5. Business Law (Agency)** | "Is the principal liable? Actual vs Apparent Authority." | **Actual** (Express/Implied) vs **Apparent** (Principal's conduct to 3rd party). | A puppet master (Principal) and puppet (Agent). "Actual" string is solid. "Apparent" string is invisible but the 3rd party sees it. |

## 🔵 **Discipline Sections (BAR / ISC / TCP)** — *The Specialist*

| Topic | The Hook | Key Concept / Mnemonic | Visual Idea |
|-------|----------|------------------------|-------------|
| **1. BAR: Cost Accounting (Variance Analysis)** | "Price Variance vs Usage Variance. Pure PURE logic." | **PURE**: **P**rice variance = (Actual **P** - Std **P**) × Actual Q. **U**sage variance = (Actual Q - Std Q) × Std P. | Formula on screen transforming. P-P × Q... Q-Q × P. "Sad face" emoji if Actual Cost > Standard Cost (Unfavorable). |
| **2. BAR: Derivatives & Hedging** | "Fair Value Hedge vs Cash Flow Hedge. Where does the gain go?" | **FV Hedge** → Income Statement. **CF Hedge** (Effective portion) → OCI (Balance Sheet). | Two buckets. "Fair Value" bucket pours straight into "Net Income." "Cash Flow" bucket pours into a reservoir called "OCI" (Equity). |
| **3. ISC: SOC Reports (Type 1 vs Type 2)** | "SOC 1 vs SOC 2. Type 1 vs Type 2. Don't mix them up." | **SOC 1**: Financial Reporting. **SOC 2**: Trust Services (Security/Privacy). **Type 1**: Design (Point in time). **Type 2**: Operating Effectiveness (Period of time). | Matrix graphic. SOC 1 (Dollar sign) vs SOC 2 (Lock icon). Type 1 (Camera snapshot) vs Type 2 (Video camera recording). |
| **4. ISC: SQL for Auditors** | "Yes, you need to know SQL. SELECT, FROM, WHERE." | **Basic Query**: SELECT [Fields], FROM [Table], WHERE [Condition], GROUP BY [Category]. | Visual of a messy file cabinet (Database). A robot arm (SQL) grabs specific folders based on commands appearing on screen. |
| **5. TCP: 1031 Exchange (Like-Kind)** | "How to defer tax on property forever. The 1031 Exchange." | **Real Property for Real Property**. Recognized Gain = Lesser of Realized Gain or Boot Received. | Visual of swapping one building for another. "Tax Man" tries to jump in, but a "1031 Barrier" blocks him. Cash (Boot) leaks out and gets taxed. |
