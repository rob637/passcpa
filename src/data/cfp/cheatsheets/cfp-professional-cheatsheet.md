# CFP Professional Conduct & Regulation Cheatsheet

## Domain Overview
- **Exam Weight**: ~9% of CFP Exam
- **Focus Areas**: CFP Board Standards, fiduciary duty, regulations, ethics, practice standards

---

## CFP Board Code of Ethics

### Core Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Integrity** | Honest and candid, even when it conflicts with self-interest |
| **Objectivity** | Unbiased advice; avoid conflicts or disclose them |
| **Competence** | Attain and maintain knowledge/skills for practice |
| **Fairness** | Fair and reasonable; balance client and professional interests |
| **Confidentiality** | Protect client information; disclose only with consent or legal requirement |
| **Professionalism** | Behave with dignity; no discrimination |
| **Diligence** | Provide services timely and thoroughly |

## Fiduciary Duty

### What It Means
- Act in client's **best interest** at all times
- Put client interests ahead of your own
- Applies to **all financial advice** provided to clients

### Components of Fiduciary Duty

| Duty | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **Duty of Loyalty** | Avoid conflicts; act solely in client interest |
| **Duty of Care** | Act with reasonable care, skill, prudence |
| **Duty to Follow Instructions** | Comply with client's lawful objectives |

### When Fiduciary Duty Applies
- All CFP professionals owe fiduciary duty when providing financial advice
- To all clients (retail and institutional)
- At all times during the relationship

### Mitigation of Conflicts
When conflicts exist:
1. Disclose the conflict
2. Obtain informed consent
3. Manage or avoid the conflict if possible

## Standards of Conduct

### Material Conflict of Interest
Must disclose and obtain informed consent for:
- Compensation that could affect objectivity
- Relationships with third parties
- Any factor that might impair professional judgment

### Scope of Engagement
- Define in writing before providing advice
- Specify products, services covered
- Can limit scope but must be reasonable
- Cannot limit to exclude client's best interest

### Informed Consent
- Clear disclosure of material facts
- Client understands and agrees
- Documented appropriately

## Financial Planning Practice Standards

### Seven Steps of Financial Planning

| Step | Actions |
|------|---------|
| 1. Understanding Circumstances | Qualify client; identify goals |
| 2. Identifying & Selecting Goals | Prioritize with client |
| 3. Analyzing Current Situation | Evaluate current position vs goals |
| 4. Developing Recommendations | Create strategies |
| 5. Presenting Recommendations | Educate client on options |
| 6. Implementing Recommendations | Execute plan with client approval |
| 7. Monitoring Progress | Review and update as needed |

### Required Elements When Providing Financial Planning

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Written Engagement | Define scope, services, compensation |
| Reasonable Basis | Recommendations supported by analysis |
| Material Disclosures | Conflicts, compensation, affiliations |
| Documentation | Keep records of advice and rationale |

## CFP Certification Requirements

### Initial Certification ("Four E's")

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Education** | Bachelor's degree + CFP Board-registered program |
| **Examination** | Pass CFP exam (170 questions, 6 hours) |
| **Experience** | 6,000 hours (or 4,000 supervised) |
| **Ethics** | Agree to Code of Ethics; background check |

### Ongoing Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| CE Hours | 30 hours every 2 years |
| Ethics CE | 2 hours (included in 30) required |
| Renewal | Biennial certification renewal |
| Background Check | Periodic verification |

### Reporting Requirements
Must report to CFP Board:
- Felony convictions
- Discipline by regulators
- Client complaints/settlements
- Bankruptcy or compromise with creditors
- Civil judgments

## Regulatory Framework

### Key Regulators

| Regulator | Jurisdiction |
|-----------|-------------|
| **SEC** | Investment advisers >$100M AUM |
| **State Securities** | Investment advisers <$100M AUM |
| **FINRA** | Broker-dealers and registered reps |
| **DOL** | ERISA retirement plans |
| **State Insurance** | Insurance products and producers |
| **CFP Board** | CFP certification standards |

### Investment Advisers Act of 1940

| Topic | Requirement |
|-------|-------------|
| Registration | With SEC or state (based on AUM threshold) |
| Form ADV | Disclosure document for clients |
| Fiduciary Duty | Duty of care and loyalty to clients |
| Custody Rule | Safeguarding client assets |
| Record Keeping | Maintain books and records |

### Form ADV

| Part | Contents |
|------|----------|
| Part 1 | Firm info (SEC and state filings) |
| Part 2A | Firm brochure (given to clients) |
| Part 2B | Brochure supplement (individual advisors) |
| Part 3 | Form CRS (relationship summary) |

## Securities Laws

### Securities Act of 1933
- **Focus**: New issues (primary market)
- Requires registration with SEC
- Prospectus requirement
- Anti-fraud provisions

### Securities Exchange Act of 1934
- **Focus**: Secondary market trading
- Created SEC
- Broker-dealer registration
- Insider trading rules
- 10b-5 anti-fraud rule

### Investment Company Act of 1940
- Regulates mutual funds
- Diversification requirements
- Board composition rules
- Fee disclosures

## Regulation Best Interest (Reg BI)

### Who It Applies To
- Broker-dealers and registered representatives
- At time of recommendation to retail customer

### Core Obligations

| Obligation | Requirement |
|------------|-------------|
| **Disclosure** | Provide Form CRS; disclose material conflicts |
| **Care** | Reasonable basis; suitable to customer |
| **Conflict of Interest** | Policies to identify and mitigate |
| **Compliance** | Policies and procedures to achieve compliance |

### Reg BI vs Fiduciary Standard
- Reg BI: Point-in-time obligation (at recommendation)
- Fiduciary: Ongoing obligation throughout relationship
- CFP professionals: Always fiduciary (higher standard)

## ERISA (Employee Retirement Income Security Act)

### Fiduciary Responsibilities
Under ERISA, fiduciaries must:
- Act solely in participants' interest
- Act for exclusive purpose of providing benefits
- Diversify investments
- Follow plan documents
- Pay only reasonable expenses

### Prohibited Transactions
- Self-dealing
- Conflicts of interest
- Transactions with parties in interest (without exemption)

### Who Is an ERISA Fiduciary?
- Anyone who:
  - Exercises discretionary authority over plan
  - Provides investment advice for fee
  - Has discretionary responsibility for administration

## Licensing Requirements

### Insurance License
- Required to sell insurance products
- State-by-state licensure
- Continuing education requirements
- Lines of authority (life, health, property, etc.)

### Series Exams (FINRA)

| Exam | Allows |
|------|--------|
| Series 6 | Limited investment products (mutual funds, variable products) |
| Series 7 | General securities representative |
| Series 63 | State law (Uniform Securities Agent) |
| Series 65 | Investment adviser representative |
| Series 66 | Combined 63 and 65 |

### Registered Investment Adviser (RIA)
- Registered with SEC or state
- Must provide Form ADV Part 2
- Ongoing fiduciary duty
- Annual updating and delivery

## Privacy Regulations

### Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA)
Financial institutions must:
- Provide privacy notice at relationship start
- Annual privacy notice (or if policy changes)
- Allow opt-out of information sharing
- Safeguard customer information

### Regulation S-P
- SEC rule implementing GLBA
- Requires written privacy policies
- Limits disclosure of NPI (nonpublic personal info)
- Opt-out provisions required

### Exception to Privacy
May share without consent:
- To complete requested transaction
- With consent
- Required by law (subpoena, court order)
- To regulatory bodies

## Anti-Money Laundering (AML)

### Bank Secrecy Act Requirements
- **Customer Identification Program (CIP)**: Verify identity
- **Customer Due Diligence (CDD)**: Know your customer
- **Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)**: Report suspicious activity
- **Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs)**: Cash transactions >$10,000

### Red Flags
- Structuring transactions to avoid reporting
- Unusual cash payments
- Third-party involvement without explanation
- Reluctance to provide information

## Compensation Disclosure

### Types of Advisor Compensation

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Fee-Only** | Client pays directly; no commissions |
| **Commission** | Paid by product provider |
| **Fee-Based** | Combination of fees and commissions |
| **Fee-Offset** | Commission credits against fee |
| **Assets Under Management** | Percentage of AUM |

### Disclosure Requirements
- All material compensation must be disclosed
- Prior to or at time of engagement
- In writing (Form ADV Part 2, fee agreement)
- Include conflicts created by compensation

## Documentation Requirements

### What to Document
- Scope of engagement
- Client information gathered
- Analysis performed
- Recommendations made and rationale
- Client decisions and instructions
- Implementation steps
- Review meetings and updates

### Retention Periods

| Record Type | Typical Retention |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Client agreements | Duration + 5 years |
| Correspondence | 5 years |
| Investment recommendations | 5 years |
| Trade records | 6 years (FINRA) |
| Advertising | 5 years |

## Marketing and Advertising Rules

### Investment Adviser Marketing Rule (SEC)

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| No false/misleading statements | Must be fair and balanced |
| Testimonials/Endorsements | Allowed with disclosures |
| Third-party ratings | Clear methodology disclosed |
| Performance advertising | Specific requirements; no cherry-picking |
| Hypothetical performance | Limited to sophisticated investors |

### General Prohibitions
- No guarantees of results
- No omission of material facts
- No misleading implications
- Balanced presentation required

## Exam Tips

1. **Fiduciary duty**: Always in client's best interest; loyalty + care
2. **Seven steps**: Know the financial planning process
3. **Form ADV**: Part 2A is client brochure; delivered before or at engagement
4. **Reg BI vs Fiduciary**: Reg BI is point-in-time; fiduciary is ongoing
5. **Privacy**: GLBA requires notice and opt-out rights
6. **AML**: $10K cash = CTR; suspicious = SAR
7. **CFP 4 E's**: Education, Exam, Experience, Ethics
8. **Compensation disclosure**: All material sources; before engagement
9. **Documentation**: Scope, rationale, recommendations, client decisions
10. **Conflicts**: Disclose, obtain consent, manage or avoid
