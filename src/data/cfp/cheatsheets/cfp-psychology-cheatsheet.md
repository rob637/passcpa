## Client Communication Fundamentals

### Active Listening Skills

| Skill | Description | Purpose |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Reflecting** | Paraphrasing client's words | Confirm understanding |
| **Clarifying** | Asking for more detail | Eliminate ambiguity |
| **Summarizing** | Recapping key points | Organize discussion |
| **Validating** | Acknowledging feelings | Build trust |

### Open vs. Closed Questions

| Type | Example | When to Use |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **Open** | "Tell me about your retirement goals" | Exploring, building rapport |
| **Closed** | "Do you have a will?" | Gathering specific facts |
| **Scaling** | "On a scale of 1-10, how important is..." | Prioritizing goals |

### Communication Styles (DISC Model)

| Style | Traits | Approach |
|-------|--------|----------|
| **Dominance** | Direct, decisive, competitive | Be brief, focus on results |
| **Influence** | Enthusiastic, optimistic, social | Build relationship, be positive |
| **Steadiness** | Patient, reliable, team-oriented | Be sincere, provide stability |
| **Conscientiousness** | Analytical, precise, systematic | Provide data, be accurate |

## Behavioral Finance Biases

### Cognitive Biases (Errors in Thinking)

| Bias | Definition | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Anchoring** | Over-relying on first information | Fixating on purchase price when deciding to sell |
| **Confirmation Bias** | Seeking info that confirms beliefs | Only reading bullish analysis on owned stocks |
| **Availability Heuristic** | Overweighting recent/memorable events | Fear of flying after plane crash news |
| **Representativeness** | Judging by stereotypes | Assuming good company = good stock |
| **Mental Accounting** | Treating money differently by source | Spending "bonus" frivolously |
| **Framing** | Decisions influenced by presentation | Preferring "90% survival" over "10% mortality" |
| **Hindsight Bias** | "I knew it all along" after the fact | Claiming to have predicted market crash |
| **Recency Bias** | Overweighting recent performance | Buying after market rise, selling after fall |
| **Overconfidence** | Overestimating one's abilities | Excessive trading, concentrated positions |
| **Illusion of Control** | Believing you can influence outcomes | Picking "lucky numbers" in lottery |

### Emotional Biases (Driven by Feelings)

| Bias | Definition | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Loss Aversion** | Losses hurt ~2x more than gains feel good | Holding losers too long to avoid realizing loss |
| **Status Quo Bias** | Preference for current state | Not rebalancing, staying in employer stock |
| **Endowment Effect** | Overvaluing what you own | Holding inherited stock beyond rational allocation |
| **Regret Aversion** | Avoiding action to prevent regret | Not selling winner fearing it goes higher |
| **Herding** | Following the crowd | Buying during bubbles, selling during panics |
| **Self-Control Bias** | Prioritizing short-term over long-term | Spending instead of saving for retirement |

### Mitigating Biases

| Strategy | Target Biases |
|----------|--------------|
| **Written Investment Policy Statement** | Overconfidence, loss aversion, herding |
| **Systematic rebalancing** | Status quo, endowment effect |
| **Dollar-cost averaging** | Anchoring, loss aversion, timing attempts |
| **Goals-based investing** | Mental accounting (positive use) |
| **Delayed decision-making** | Recency, availability, emotional biases |

## Money Scripts

### Four Money Script Types (Klontz)

| Script | Belief | Associated Behaviors |
|--------|--------|---------------------|
| **Money Avoidance** | Money is bad, rich are greedy | Under-earning, self-sabotage, giving away money |
| **Money Worship** | Money solves all problems | Overspending, hoarding, workaholism |
| **Money Status** | Self-worth = net worth | Keeping up with Joneses, overspending on image |
| **Money Vigilance** | Money is private, be alert | Frugality, anxiety about money, secretive |

> **Key Insight:** Money scripts develop in childhood and influence adult financial behavior. Awareness is the first step to change.

## Client-Planner Relationship

### Building Trust

| Factor | Implementation |
|--------|---------------|
| **Competence** | Credentials, continuing education |
| **Integrity** | Fiduciary duty, transparency |
| **Benevolence** | Client-first focus, genuine care |
| **Reliability** | Consistent follow-through |

### Stages of Change (Transtheoretical Model)

| Stage | Client Behavior | Planner Approach |
|-------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Precontemplation** | Not aware of need to change | Raise awareness, provide information |
| **Contemplation** | Considering change | Discuss pros/cons, explore ambivalence |
| **Preparation** | Ready to take action | Set goals, create action plan |
| **Action** | Actively changing | Implement plan, provide support |
| **Maintenance** | Sustaining change | Monitor progress, prevent relapse |

A significant life event that shapes money beliefs:
- Parents' divorce over money

## Crisis and Life Transitions

### Major Life Events Requiring Financial Counseling

| Event | Key Financial Considerations |
|-------|------------------------------|
| **Death of spouse** | Income replacement, estate settlement, emotional decisions |
| **Divorce** | Asset division, QDRO, lifestyle adjustments |
| **Job loss** | Severance, COBRA, emergency fund, retirement plan decisions |
| **Sudden wealth** | Lottery, inheritance, overwhelming choices |
| **Disability** | Income replacement, benefits, healthcare costs |
| **Retirement** | Identity, purpose, spending adjustment |

### Counseling Approach for Crisis

1. **Acknowledge emotions** (don't rush to problem-solving)
2. **Stabilize situation** (address immediate needs)
3. **Avoid major decisions** during acute grief
4. **Create breathing room** (short-term plan)
5. **Long-term planning** when emotionally ready

## Risk Tolerance Assessment

### Components of Risk Capacity vs. Risk Tolerance

| Factor | Risk Capacity | Risk Tolerance |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| **Definition** | Ability to take risk | Willingness to take risk |
| **Measures** | Time horizon, income stability, net worth | Psychological comfort with volatility |
| **Objective** | Yes | No (subjective) |
| **Example** | Long timeline = high capacity | May still prefer bonds |

### Risk Tolerance Questionnaires

- Standard questionnaires measure risk tolerance (willingness)
- Must also assess risk capacity (ability) and risk need (required return)
- **All three should be considered** in portfolio construction

## Client Demographics and Cultural Considerations

### Generational Differences

| Generation | Birth Years | Financial Tendencies |
|------------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Silent** | 1928-1945 | Conservative, savers, loyal |
| **Boomers** | 1946-1964 | Work-focused, optimistic, debt-comfortable |
| **Gen X** | 1965-1980 | Independent, skeptical, pragmatic |
| **Millennials** | 1981-1996 | Values-driven, digital, student debt |
| **Gen Z** | 1997-2012 | Entrepreneurial, mobile-first, financially aware |

### Cultural Competence

- Be aware of cultural differences in money attitudes
- Some cultures prefer family involvement in financial decisions
- Avoid assumptions; ask about preferences
- Understand roles and expectations vary by culture

## Key Terms for the Exam

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Affect heuristic** | Decisions based on current emotions |
| **Bounded rationality** | Decisions limited by information and time |
| **Cognitive dissonance** | Discomfort from conflicting beliefs |
| **Prospect theory** | Losses loom larger than gains (Kahneman/Tversky) |
| **Satisficing** | Choosing "good enough" vs. optimal |
| **Sunk cost fallacy** | Continuing due to prior investment |
| **System 1/System 2** | Fast intuitive vs. slow deliberate thinking |

## Exam Tips

1. **Know all cognitive AND emotional biases** by name and example
2. **Understand difference between risk capacity and risk tolerance**
3. **Recognize stages of change** for client readiness
4. **Match communication style** to client personality
5. **Crisis events** require emotional acknowledgment before financial planning
6. **Money scripts** explain irrational financial behaviors
