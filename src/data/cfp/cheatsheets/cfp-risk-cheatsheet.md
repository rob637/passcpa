# CFP Risk Management & Insurance Cheatsheet

## Domain Overview
- **Exam Weight**: ~12% of CFP Exam
- **Focus Areas**: Risk management process, life/health/disability/property insurance, Medicare, Social Security integration

---

## Risk Management Process

### Five Steps
1. **Identify** risks and exposures
2. **Analyze** and evaluate severity/frequency
3. **Select** risk management technique
4. **Implement** the chosen strategy
5. **Monitor** and reassess periodically

### Risk Management Techniques

| Technique | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Avoidance** | Eliminate the risk entirely | Don't buy a motorcycle |
| **Reduction** | Lower probability or severity | Install smoke detectors |
| **Retention** | Accept and self-insure | High deductible plan |
| **Transfer** | Shift risk to another party | Purchase insurance |

### When to Use Each
- **High Frequency + High Severity**: Avoid
- **High Frequency + Low Severity**: Reduce/Retain
- **Low Frequency + High Severity**: Transfer (insure)
- **Low Frequency + Low Severity**: Retain

## Insurance Fundamentals

### Key Principles

| Principle | Definition |
|-----------|------------|
| **Insurable Interest** | Must have financial stake in insured property/life |
| **Indemnity** | Restore to pre-loss condition (no profit) |
| **Subrogation** | Insurer's right to pursue third party |
| **Utmost Good Faith** | Full disclosure required |
| **Adhesion** | Contract written by insurer; ambiguity favors insured |
| **Contribution** | Multiple policies share loss proportionally |

### Aleatory Contract
- Unequal exchange of value possible
- Premium paid may far exceed or be less than benefits received

## Life Insurance

### Term Life Insurance

| Type | Features |
|------|----------|
| **Level Term** | Fixed premium and death benefit |
| **Annual Renewable (ART)** | Increasing premium; renewable without evidence |
| **Decreasing Term** | Declining death benefit; level premium |
| **Return of Premium** | Returns premiums if outlive term |

**Best For**: Temporary needs, maximum coverage, limited budget

### Permanent Life Insurance

| Type | Key Features |
|------|--------------|
| **Whole Life** | Fixed premium; guaranteed cash value growth |
| **Universal Life** | Flexible premiums; interest-credited cash value |
| **Variable Life** | Fixed premium; investment subaccounts |
| **Variable Universal (VUL)** | Flexible premium + investment subaccounts |
| **Indexed Universal (IUL)** | Cash value tied to index (with floor) |

### Cash Value Accumulation
- Tax-deferred growth
- Access via policy loans (typically tax-free)
- Surrender for cash value (taxable gain)
- Basis = premiums paid

### Life Insurance Needs Methods

| Method | Approach |
|--------|----------|
| **Human Life Value** | PV of future earnings |
| **Capital Needs** | Amount to generate needed income |
| **Multiple of Income** | 5-10× annual income (rough) |
| **Needs Analysis** | Itemize obligations and assets |

## Life Insurance Taxation

### Income Tax Treatment

| Event | Tax Treatment |
|-------|---------------|
| Death Benefit | Generally income tax-free |
| Cash Value Growth | Tax-deferred |
| Policy Loan | Tax-free (if policy stays in force) |
| Surrender | Gain taxable as ordinary income |
| 1035 Exchange | Tax-free exchange of policies |

### Modified Endowment Contract (MEC)
- Fails 7-pay test (funded too quickly)
- Loans/withdrawals taxable + 10% penalty before 59½
- Death benefit still income tax-free
- Subject to LIFO taxation

### Estate Tax
- Death benefit included in estate if:
  - Owner is insured, OR
  - Deceased had incidents of ownership
- **ILIT** (Irrevocable Life Insurance Trust) removes from estate

## Disability Insurance

### Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Own Occupation** | Cannot perform own specific job |
| **Any Occupation** | Cannot perform any job suited to education/training |
| **Presumptive Disability** | Automatic qualification (loss of sight, limbs, etc.) |
| **Residual Disability** | Partial disability; proportional benefits |

### Policy Features

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Elimination Period** | Waiting period before benefits begin (30-180 days) |
| **Benefit Period** | How long benefits last (2 years to age 65/lifetime) |
| **Benefit Amount** | Typically 60-70% of gross income |
| **Non-cancelable** | Cannot change terms or premiums |
| **Guaranteed Renewable** | Must renew but premiums may increase by class |

### Riders
- **COLA**: Benefits increase with inflation
- **Future Increase Option**: Buy more coverage without evidence
- **Own Occupation**: Maintain own-occ definition
- **Waiver of Premium**: Premiums waived during disability

### Taxation of Benefits
| Premium Paid By | Benefits Received |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Employer (not taxed to employee) | Taxable |
| Employee (after-tax) | Tax-free |
| Employer (taxed to employee) | Tax-free |

## Long-Term Care Insurance

### What It Covers
- Nursing home care
- Assisted living facilities
- Home health care
- Adult day care

### Benefit Triggers (2 of 6 ADLs OR Cognitive Impairment)
1. Bathing
2. Dressing
3. Toileting
4. Transferring
5. Continence
6. Eating

### Key Policy Terms

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Daily/Monthly Benefit** | Maximum coverage per day/month |
| **Benefit Period** | Years of coverage (2-6 years or lifetime) |
| **Elimination Period** | Waiting period (30-90 days typical) |
| **Inflation Protection** | Simple vs compound increases |

### Tax-Qualified Policies
- Premiums deductible as medical expense (age limits)
- Benefits generally tax-free up to per diem limit ($420/day 2024)
- Must meet benefit triggers

### 2024 Deductible Limits (Based on Age)

| Age | Maximum Deduction |
|-----|-------------------|
| 40 and under | $480 |
| 41-50 | $890 |
| 51-60 | $1,790 |
| 61-70 | $4,770 |
| 71+ | $5,960 |

## Health Insurance

### Plan Types

| Type | Features |
|------|----------|
| **PPO** | Network flexibility; no referrals needed; higher premiums |
| **HMO** | Gatekeeper PCP; lower cost; limited network |
| **EPO** | PPO-like but no out-of-network coverage |
| **POS** | Hybrid HMO/PPO; PCP referrals for specialists |
| **HDHP** | High deductible; HSA eligible |

### High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) 2024

| Feature | Self-Only | Family |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| Minimum Deductible | $1,600 | $3,200 |
| Maximum Out-of-Pocket | $8,050 | $16,100 |

### Health Savings Account (HSA) 2024

| Feature | Self-Only | Family |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| Contribution Limit | $4,150 | $8,300 |
| Catch-up (55+) | +$1,000 | +$1,000 |

**HSA Benefits**:
- Triple tax advantage (deductible, tax-free growth, tax-free qualified withdrawals)
- No use-it-or-lose-it
- Portable
- Investment options available
- After 65: non-medical withdrawals taxable (no penalty)

### Flexible Spending Account (FSA)
- **2024 Limit**: $3,200
- Use-it-or-lose-it (or $640 carryover if employer allows)
- Pre-tax contributions
- Employer-sponsored only

### COBRA Continuation
- 18 months (qualifying event: job loss, reduced hours)
- 29 months (if disabled at time of event)
- 36 months (divorce, death of employee, Medicare entitlement)
- Premium: Up to 102% of full cost

## Medicare

### Part Overview

| Part | Coverage | 2024 Premium |
|------|----------|--------------|
| **Part A** | Inpatient hospital, SNF, hospice | $0 (with 40 quarters) |
| **Part B** | Outpatient, doctor visits | $174.70/month |
| **Part C** | Medicare Advantage (private plans) | Varies |
| **Part D** | Prescription drugs | Varies |

### Part A Cost Sharing (2024)

| Benefit Period Days | Cost |
|--------------------|------|
| 1-60 | $1,632 deductible |
| 61-90 | $408/day coinsurance |
| 91-150 (lifetime reserve) | $816/day |
| 150+ | Full cost |

### Part B Cost Sharing
- $240 annual deductible
- 20% coinsurance after deductible
- No out-of-pocket maximum

### Enrollment Periods

| Period | Timing | Coverage Start |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Initial Enrollment | 7 months around 65th birthday | Month after enrollment |
| General Enrollment | Jan 1 - Mar 31 | July 1 |
| Open Enrollment (MA) | Oct 15 - Dec 7 | Jan 1 |
| Late Enrollment Penalty | Part B: 10%/year late; Part D: 1%/month late | Ongoing |

### Medigap (Medicare Supplement)
- Standardized plans A-N
- Supplements Original Medicare
- Cannot use with Medicare Advantage
- Guaranteed issue: 6 months after Part B enrollment at 65+
- Plans C and F closed to new enrollees after 2020

## Property & Casualty Insurance

### Homeowners Forms

| Form | Coverage |
|------|----------|
| **HO-1** | Basic (named perils) |
| **HO-2** | Broad (more perils) |
| **HO-3** | Special (open perils on dwelling) |
| **HO-4** | Renters |
| **HO-5** | Comprehensive (open perils all) |
| **HO-6** | Condo |
| **HO-8** | Older homes (market value) |

### HO-3 Coverage Parts

| Coverage | Description | Typical % |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **A** | Dwelling | Base amount |
| **B** | Other Structures | 10% of A |
| **C** | Personal Property | 50% of A |
| **D** | Loss of Use | 20% of A |
| **E** | Personal Liability | $100K+ |
| **F** | Medical Payments | $1K-$5K |

### Coinsurance Requirement
- Must insure at 80% of replacement cost
- Penalty if underinsured:
```
Payout = (Amount Carried / Amount Required) × Loss
```

### Auto Insurance

| Coverage | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Liability** | Bodily injury/property damage to others |
| **Collision** | Your car damaged in collision |
| **Comprehensive** | Your car damaged by non-collision |
| **Uninsured Motorist** | Other driver has no insurance |
| **Underinsured Motorist** | Other driver has insufficient coverage |
| **Medical Payments** | Medical expenses regardless of fault |
| **PIP** | No-fault medical/wage coverage |

### Umbrella Policy
- Excess liability above underlying policies
- Typically $1M+ coverage
- Requires minimum underlying limits
- Covers broadly (including some excluded by underlying)

## Social Security Disability (SSDI)

### Eligibility
- Work credits (varies by age)
- "Disability" = cannot perform substantial gainful activity
- Expected to last 12+ months or result in death
- 5-month waiting period

### 2024 Substantial Gainful Activity
- Non-blind: $1,550/month
- Blind: $2,590/month

### Benefit Amount
- Based on AIME (same as retirement)
- No reduction for early claiming
- Converts to retirement at FRA

## Annuities

### Types

| Type | Features |
|------|----------|
| **Immediate** | Income begins within 1 year |
| **Deferred** | Income begins in future |
| **Fixed** | Guaranteed interest rate |
| **Variable** | Returns based on subaccounts |
| **Indexed** | Returns tied to market index |

### Taxation
- Tax-deferred growth
- LIFO for withdrawals (earnings first)
- Ordinary income on gains
- 10% penalty before 59½
- Exclusion ratio for immediate annuities

### Payout Options
- **Life Only**: Highest payment; ends at death
- **Life with Period Certain**: Payments for life or minimum period
- **Joint and Survivor**: Continues to surviving spouse

## Exam Tips

1. **Risk matrix**: Know when to avoid, reduce, retain, or transfer
2. **Insurable interest timing**: Life = inception; Property = at loss
3. **Own vs Any occupation**: Own-occ is more favorable
4. **MEC rules**: 7-pay test; LIFO taxation + 10% penalty
5. **Disability taxation**: Who pays premium = who gets taxed
6. **LTC triggers**: 2 of 6 ADLs or cognitive impairment
7. **Medicare parts**: Know A/B/C/D coverage and costs
8. **IRMAA**: Based on 2-year prior MAGI
9. **HO-3**: Open perils on dwelling, named perils on contents
10. **Coinsurance**: 80% requirement; penalty formula
