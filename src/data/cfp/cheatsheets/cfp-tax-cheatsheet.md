## Income Categories

### Gross Income Includes
- Wages, salaries, tips
- Interest and dividends
- Business income
- Capital gains
- IRA/pension distributions
- Rental income
- Alimony (pre-2019 divorces)
- Gambling winnings
- Forgiven debt (exceptions apply)

### Exclusions from Income
- Municipal bond interest
- Life insurance death proceeds
- Gifts and inheritances
- Child support
- Qualified Roth distributions
- First $500K gain on home (MFJ)
- Workers' compensation
- Scholarships (for tuition)

### AGI vs MAGI
- **AGI** = Gross Income - Above-the-line deductions
- **MAGI** = AGI + certain add-backs (varies by provision)

## Itemized Deductions (From AGI)

### Medical Expenses
- **Threshold**: Exceeds 7.5% of AGI
- Includes: Doctor, dental, vision, prescriptions, insurance premiums, long-term care (limits)

### State and Local Taxes (SALT)
- **Cap**: $10,000 total ($5,000 MFS)
- Includes: State/local income OR sales tax + property taxes

### Mortgage Interest
- **Acquisition debt limit**: $750,000 ($375,000 MFS)
- Grandfathered at $1M if originated before 12/16/2017
- Home equity interest: Only deductible if used to buy/build/improve home

### Charitable Contributions
| Type | Cash Limit | Property Limit |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| Public charities | 60% of AGI | 30% LTCG / 50% cost |
| Private foundations | 30% of AGI | 20% LTCG |
| 5-year carryforward for excess contributions

### Casualty/Theft Losses
- Only federally declared disasters
- $100 floor + 10% AGI threshold

### Nonrefundable Credits (reduce tax to $0)

| Credit | Amount | Income Phase-out |
|--------|--------|------------------|
| Child Tax Credit | $2,000/child under 17 | $200K/$400K (MFJ) |
| Dependent Care | 20-35% of $3,000/$6,000 | Reduced at >$15K AGI |
| Lifetime Learning | 20% of $10,000 | $80K-$90K / $160K-$180K |
| Retirement Saver's | 10-50% of $2,000 | Phase-out at low income |
| Adoption Credit | Up to $16,810 | Phase-out starts $252,150 |

### Refundable Credits (can exceed tax)

| Credit | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Earned Income Credit | Up to $7,830 (3+ children) |
| Additional Child Tax Credit | Up to $1,700 refundable |
| American Opportunity Credit | $2,500 (40% refundable) |

### American Opportunity vs Lifetime Learning

| Feature | AOTC | LLC |
|---------|------|-----|

## Capital Gains & Losses

### Holding Period
- **Short-term**: ≤1 year (ordinary rates)
- **Long-term**: >1 year (preferential rates)

### Long-Term Capital Gains Rates (2026)

| Rate | Single | MFJ |
|------|--------|-----|
| 0% | $0 - $48,350 | $0 - $96,700 |
| 15% | $48,351 - $533,400 | $96,701 - $600,050 |
| 20% | Over $533,400 | Over $600,050 |

### Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT)
- **3.8%** additional tax on lesser of:
  - Net investment income, OR
  - MAGI over threshold ($200K single / $250K MFJ)

### Loss Deduction
- Net up to **$3,000** against ordinary income ($1,500 MFS)
- Unlimited carryforward of unused losses

### Wash Sale Rule
- Cannot deduct loss if substantially identical security purchased within **30 days** before or after sale
- Basis adjustment applies

## Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT)

### 2026 Exemption

| Status | Exemption | Phase-out Start |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|  
| Single | $90,200 | $626,350 |
| MFJ | $140,500 | $1,252,700 |
| MFS | $70,250 | $626,350 |

### AMT Rates
- **26%** on AMTI up to $239,100 ($119,550 MFS)
- **28%** on AMTI above threshold

### Common AMT Adjustments
- **Add back**: SALT deduction, personal exemptions, misc deductions
- **Preference items**: Private activity bond interest, ISO exercise spread
- **No adjustment**: Charitable contributions, medical expenses, mortgage interest (acquisition)

## Self-Employment Tax

### 2026 Rules
- **15.3%** on first $176,100 (12.4% SS + 2.9% Medicare)
- **2.9%** Medicare only above $176,100
- **0.9%** Additional Medicare Tax over $200K/$250K

### Deduction
- 50% of SE tax is above-the-line deduction
- Net SE income × 92.35% × 15.3%

## Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction

### Section 199A Basics
- **20%** deduction of qualified business income
- Applies to pass-through entities (S-corps, partnerships, sole proprietors)

### Income Limits (2026)

| Status | Full Deduction | Phase-out Range |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|  
| Single | Below $197,300 | $197,300 - $247,300 |
| MFJ | Below $394,600 | $394,600 - $494,600 |

### SSTB Limitation
- Specified Service Trades/Businesses (law, health, consulting, etc.)
- Full deduction phases out within thresholds
- No deduction above upper threshold

### W-2/Property Limitation
Above thresholds: Lesser of:
- 50% of W-2 wages, OR
- 25% of W-2 wages + 2.5% of qualified property

## Passive Activity Rules

### Definition
- Trade/business where taxpayer doesn't materially participate
- Rental activities (presumed passive)

### Material Participation Tests (any one)
1. 500+ hours during year
2. Substantially all participation
3. More than 100 hours and not less than any other
4. 500+ hours over prior 5 years (with 3 qualifying)
5. Personal service activity in any 3 prior years
6. Facts and circumstances
7. More than 100 hours of regular, continuous participation

### PAL Rules
- Passive losses offset only passive income
- Excess losses suspended and carried forward
- Released upon complete disposition

### Real Estate Professional Exception
- 750+ hours in real property trades
- More than 50% of personal services
- Material participation in each activity

### $25,000 Rental Loss Allowance
- Active participation required
- Phase-out: $100K-$150K MAGI
- Not available if MAGI >$150K

## Gift and Estate Tax Integration

### Annual Gift Exclusion
- **$19,000** per donee (2026)
- **$38,000** married couple (gift splitting)
- Unlimited: Medical/education paid directly

### Lifetime Exemption
- **$7.0 million** per person (2026, post-TCJA sunset)
- **$14.0 million** per married couple (with portability)

### Gift Tax Rate
- **40%** on gifts exceeding lifetime exemption

### Basis Rules
| Transfer | Basis |
|----------|-------|
| Gift | Carryover (donor's basis) |
| Inheritance | Stepped-up (FMV at death) |

## Exam Tips

1. **Know 2026 numbers**: Brackets, standard deduction, credit amounts
2. **Filing status order**: HOH > MFJ split > Single (usually)
3. **Above vs below the line**: AGI impact for other deductions
4. **SALT cap**: $10,000 is key limitation
5. **Capital gain math**: Netting ST vs LT, $3,000 loss limit
6. **NIIT threshold**: $200K/$250K - memorize
7. **AMT triggers**: ISO exercise, large SALT, private activity bonds
8. **QBI limitations**: Know SSTB and W-2 wage tests
9. **Passive activity**: 7 material participation tests
10. **Gift basis vs inheritance basis**: Carryover vs step-up
