# Social Security Quick Reference

## Full Retirement Age (FRA)

| Birth Year | Full Retirement Age |
|------------|---------------------|
| 1943-1954 | 66 |
| 1955 | 66 + 2 months |
| 1956 | 66 + 4 months |
| 1957 | 66 + 6 months |
| 1958 | 66 + 8 months |
| 1959 | 66 + 10 months |
| 1960+ | 67 |

---

## Claiming Ages

| Age | Effect |
|-----|--------|
| 62 | First eligible - maximum reduction |
| FRA | Full benefit (Primary Insurance Amount) |
| 70 | Maximum benefit (delayed credits stop) |

### Early Filing Reduction

| Months Early | Reduction per Month |
|--------------|---------------------|
| First 36 months | 5/9 of 1% |
| Beyond 36 months | 5/12 of 1% |

**At 62 (for FRA 67)**: ~30% reduction

### Delayed Retirement Credits

- 8% increase per year past FRA
- Maximum at age 70
- **35% increase** for FRA 67 waiting to 70

---

## Benefit Calculation

### Premium Insurance Amount (PIA)
Based on Average Indexed Monthly Earnings (AIME)

| AIME Bracket | Replacement Rate |
|--------------|------------------|
| First $1,174 | 90% |
| $1,174 - $7,078 | 32% |
| Above $7,078 | 15% |

(Bend points adjusted annually)

### 35-Year Rule
- PIA based on 35 highest earning years
- Years with $0 earnings count as zeros
- Work longer to replace zero years

---

## Spousal Benefits

### Spouse (at FRA)
- **50%** of worker's PIA
- Reduced if claimed early
- Cannot receive own + spousal (get higher of two)

### Divorced Spouse
Requirements:
- Marriage lasted 10+ years
- Currently unmarried
- Both at least 62
- Can file independently if divorced 2+ years

---

## Survivor Benefits

### Widow(er) Benefits

| Age | Benefit |
|-----|---------|
| 60 | Reduced (71.5% of PIA) |
| FRA | 100% of deceased's benefit |
| Disabled (50+) | 71.5% of PIA |

### Other Survivors
- Dependent children: Up to 75% of PIA
- Family Maximum: 150-180% of PIA

### Remarriage Rules
| Remarries at | Effect |
|--------------|--------|
| Before 60 | Loses benefits (unless later divorce/death) |
| 60 or after | Keeps survivor benefits |

## Earnings Test

### Under Full Retirement Age
| Year | Limit | Withholding |
|------|-------|-------------|
| 2026 | $23,400 | $1 for every $2 over |

### Year of FRA (before month reaching FRA)
| Year | Limit | Withholding |
|------|-------|-------------|
| 2026 | $62,160 | $1 for every $3 over |

### At FRA and Beyond
- No earnings test
- No withholding

**Note**: Withheld benefits are returned after FRA

---

## Taxation of Benefits

### Provisional Income Calculation
Provisional = AGI + Tax-Exempt Interest + 50% of SS Benefits

### Single Filers

| Provisional Income | % Taxable |
|-------------------|-----------|
| Below $25,000 | 0% |
| $25,000 - $34,000 | Up to 50% |
| Above $34,000 | Up to 85% |

### Married Filing Jointly

| Provisional Income | % Taxable |
|-------------------|-----------|
| Below $32,000 | 0% |
| $32,000 - $44,000 | Up to 50% |
| Above $44,000 | Up to 85% |

---

## Disability Benefits (SSDI)

### Eligibility
- "Work credits" based on work history
- 40 credits total, 20 in last 10 years (typical)
- Meet SSA definition of disability

### Definition
- Unable to engage in Substantial Gainful Activity (SGA)
- Medical condition expected to last 12+ months or result in death

### 2026 SGA Limit
- Non-blind: ~$1,620/month
- Blind: ~$2,700/month

## Key Planning Points

1. **Break-even age**: ~82 for early vs FRA, ~85 for FRA vs 70
2. **Married couples**: Coordinate for optimal strategy
3. **File & suspend**: Eliminated (but restricted application exists)
4. **Medicare**: Starts at 65 regardless of SS claiming
5. **Windfall Elimination**: Affects those with non-covered pensions
