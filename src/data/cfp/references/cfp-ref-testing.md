# CFP Exam Information

## Exam Overview

The CFP® exam tests your ability to apply financial planning knowledge to real-world client situations using case study and standalone questions.

---

## Exam Structure

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Total Questions | 170 questions |
| Format | Multiple choice |
| Time | 6 hours total |
| Sessions | 2 sessions (3 hours each) |
| Break | 40-minute break between sessions |

### Question Distribution

| Session | Questions | Time |
|---------|-----------|------|
| Morning | 85 questions | 3 hours |
| Afternoon | 85 questions | 3 hours |

## Case Study Format

### Case Questions (~60% of exam)
- Multi-part scenarios
- 2-3 pages of client information
- 8-12 questions per case
- Tests integration of knowledge

### Standalone Questions (~40% of exam)
- Independent questions
- Tests specific knowledge points
- May reference brief scenarios

---

## Passing Score

- **Criterion-referenced scoring**
- Not a fixed percentage (varies by exam version)
- Most pass rates: **60-70%** historically
- Pass rate: Approximately 65% for first-time takers

## Exam Day Tips

1. **Get good sleep** the night before
2. **Eat breakfast** - you need energy
3. **Arrive early** - 30 minutes before start
4. **Pace yourself** - ~2 minutes per question
5. **Flag and return** - don't get stuck
6. **Use the break** - eat, stretch, refocus
7. **Stay calm** - second session often easier
8. **Answer everything** - no penalty for guessing
