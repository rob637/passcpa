# IPPF (International Professional Practices Framework)

## Overview

The International Professional Practices Framework (IPPF) is the authoritative framework for internal auditing, published by The IIA (Institute of Internal Auditors).

---

## IPPF Structure

### Mandatory Guidance

Must be followed to practice internal auditing:

| Component | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| **Core Principles** | Foundation for internal audit practice |
| **Definition of IA** | Defines internal auditing |
| **Code of Ethics** | Behavioral expectations |
| **Standards** | Requirements for practice |

### Recommended Guidance

Endorsed best practices:

| Component | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| **Implementation Guidance** | How to apply Standards |
| **Supplemental Guidance** | Specialized topics |

---

## Core Principles for Internal Auditing

The 10 Core Principles that underpin effective internal auditing:

1. **Demonstrates integrity**
2. **Demonstrates competence and due professional care**
3. **Is objective and free from undue influence**
4. **Aligns with strategies, objectives, and risks**
5. **Is appropriately positioned and adequately resourced**
6. **Demonstrates quality and continuous improvement**
7. **Communicates effectively**
8. **Provides risk-based assurance**
9. **Is insightful, proactive, and future-focused**
10. **Promotes organizational improvement**

---

## Definition of Internal Auditing

>"Internal auditing is an independent, objective assurance and consulting activity designed to add value and improve an organization's operations. It helps an organization accomplish its objectives by bringing a systematic, disciplined approach to evaluate and improve the effectiveness of risk management, control, and governance processes."

## Key Standards to Know

### 1000 - Purpose, Authority, Responsibility
- Internal audit charter must be established
- Charter defines purpose and responsibilities
- Must be approved by the board

### 1100 - Independence and Objectivity
- Organizational independence
- CAE reports functionally to board
- Individual objectivity of auditors

### 1300 - QAIP
- Quality Assurance and Improvement Program
- Internal assessments (ongoing + periodic)
- External assessments (at least every 5 years)

### 2100 - Nature of Work
- Evaluate governance
- Evaluate risk management
- Evaluate control processes

### 2400 - Communicating Results
- Must include objectives and scope
- Include conclusions and recommendations
- Opinion on adequacy of controls

## Three Lines Model

The IIA's model for organizational governance:

| Line | Role | Responsibility |
|------|------|----------------|
| **First Line** | Operational Management | Own and manage risks |
| **Second Line** | Risk & Compliance Functions | Support risk management |
| **Third Line** | Internal Audit | Independent assurance |

**Governing Body** oversees all three lines

---

## Exam Tips

1. Know the **mandatory vs recommended** distinction
2. **Attribute = who**, **Performance = what**
3. CAE reports **functionally to board**, administratively to executive
4. External QA assessment **every 5 years minimum**
5. Internal audit **adds value and improves operations**
