# CPA REG Cheatsheet

## Section Overview
- **Exam Weight**: 25% of Core Exam
- **Focus Areas**: Individual taxation, business taxation, entity selection, ethics/law
- **Time**: Allocated ~93 minutes in Core (plus TBS time)

---

## Individual Taxation

### Filing Status Priority
1. Married Filing Jointly (MFJ) - Generally most favorable
2. Qualifying Surviving Spouse (QSS) - 2 years after spouse death
3. Head of Household (HOH) - Unmarried + qualified person + 50%+ home costs
4. Single
5. Married Filing Separately (MFS) - Least favorable

### Gross Income Formula
```
Gross Income
- Above-the-Line Deductions (For AGI)
= Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)
- Standard Deduction OR Itemized Deductions
- QBI Deduction (if applicable)
= Taxable Income
× Tax Rates
= Tax Liability
- Credits
= Tax Due/Refund
```

## Dependents

### Qualifying Child - **CARES**
- **C**lose relative (child, sibling, etc.)
- **A**ge: Under 19, or 24 if full-time student, or any age if disabled
- **R**esidency: Lives with taxpayer > 6 months
- **E**liminate: Cannot provide > 50% of own support
- **S**ingle: Cannot file joint return (unless for refund only)

### Qualifying Relative - **SUPORT**
- **S**upport: Taxpayer provides > 50%
- **U**nder threshold: Gross income < $5,250 (2026)
- **P**recludes dependency: Not a qualifying child of anyone
- **O**nly citizen/resident: U.S. citizen, resident, or national
- **R**elation: Related or member of household
- **T**ax return: Not filing joint (unless for refund only)

## Itemized Deductions

### Medical Expenses
- Threshold: > 7.5% of AGI
- Includes: Medical care, insurance premiums, long-term care (limited)

### SALT (State and Local Taxes)
- Cap: $10,000 ($5,000 MFS)
- Includes: State income OR sales + property taxes

### Mortgage Interest
- Acquisition debt limit: $750,000 ($375,000 MFS)
- Home equity: Only if used to buy/build/improve home

### Charitable Contributions
| Type | Cash | Property |
|------|------|----------|
| Public charity | 60% AGI | 30% AGI (LTCG) |
| Private foundation | 30% AGI | 20% AGI |

## Capital Gains & Losses

### Holding Period
- **Short-term**: ≤ 1 year (ordinary rates)
- **Long-term**: > 1 year (preferential rates: 0%/15%/20%)

### 2026 LTCG Rate Thresholds
| Rate | Single | MFJ |
|------|--------|-----|
| 0% | Up to $49,600 | Up to $99,200 |
| 15% | $49,601 - $495,200 | $99,201 - $555,350 |
| 20% | Over $495,200 | Over $555,350 |

### Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT)
- 3.8% on lesser of: NII or excess MAGI over threshold
- Thresholds: $200,000 (Single), $250,000 (MFJ)

### §1231 Assets (Business Use > 1 Year)
- Net gain → Long-term capital gain
- Net loss → Ordinary loss
- 5-year lookback for prior §1231 losses

## Business Entity Taxation

### C Corporation
- Entity pays tax at 21% flat rate
- Dividends taxed again to shareholders (double taxation)
- Reasonable compensation required

### S Corporation
- Pass-through taxation (no entity-level tax)
- Requirements: ≤100 shareholders, US individuals/trusts, one class of stock
- Shareholder basis: Stock basis + Debt basis (personally loaned)
- Distributions taxed when exceed basis

### Partnership
- Pass-through taxation
- Partners include share of liabilities in basis
- Distributions generally not taxable unless exceed basis
- Built-in gains/losses tracked for contributing partners

### Sole Proprietorship
- Reported on Schedule C
- Subject to self-employment tax
- No separate entity recognition

## Basis Rules

### Property Received by Gift
- **General rule**: Donor's basis (carryover)
- **Loss property exception**: If FMV < basis at gift date
  - For gain: Use donor's basis
  - For loss: Use FMV at gift date
  - Between: No gain/loss

### Property Received by Inheritance
- **General rule**: Step up (or down) to FMV at date of death
- **Alternate valuation**: 6 months after death (if elected for entire estate)

### Like-Kind Exchange (§1031)
- Real property only (no personal property)
- Basis = Old basis + boot paid - boot received + gain recognized
- Boot received triggers gain (not losses)

## Business Deductions

### §179 Expense (2026)
- Maximum: $1,220,000
- Phase-out begins: $3,050,000 of qualifying property
- Must be profitable (limited to business income)

### Bonus Depreciation (2026)
- 40% (phasing down 20% per year)
- No taxable income limitation
- Available for new AND used property

### Business Interest Limitation (§163(j))
- Limited to 30% of ATI (adjusted taxable income)
- Exceptions: Small business (≤$30M gross receipts), real estate election

---

## Like-Kind Exchange vs Involuntary Conversion

| Feature | §1031 Like-Kind | §1033 Involuntary |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Property type | Real property only | Any property |
| Intent | Voluntary exchange | Involuntary (destruction, theft, condemnation) |
| Timeline | 45-day ID, 180-day close | 2-3 years to replace |
| Recognition | Boot = gain | Excess proceeds = gain |

## Ethics & Professional Responsibilities

### Circular 230 Covered Opinions
- Written advice concerning federal tax issues
- Penalties for incompetence or disreputable conduct
- Must use due diligence

### Preparer Penalties
| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Unreasonable position | Greater of $1,000 or 50% of income |
| Willful/reckless | Greater of $5,000 or 75% of income |
| Failure to sign | $60 per return |
| Failure to provide copy | $60 per return |

### Penalties - Taxpayers
| Penalty | Rate |
|---------|------|
| Failure to file | 5%/month (max 25%) |
| Failure to pay | 0.5%/month (max 25%) |
| Accuracy-related | 20% of underpayment |
| Fraud | 75% of underpayment |

## Common Exam Traps

1. **Gift basis**: Must check for loss property rule
2. **§1031**: Real property ONLY since 2018
3. **S-Corp basis**: Only shareholder LOANS add basis (not guaranteed corp debt)
4. **Partnership basis**: Includes share of ALL partnership liabilities
5. **QBI deduction**: Limited for specified service trades (SSTB) above thresholds
6. **Alimony**: Pre-2019 = deductible/taxable; Post-2018 = neither
7. **Kiddie tax**: Unearned income > $2,600 taxed at parent's rate
8. **HOH**: Requires paying >50% of household costs
