# AICPA Code of Professional Conduct

## Overview

The AICPA Code of Professional Conduct establishes ethical requirements for CPAs. The code applies to all AICPA members and is the foundation for ethical practice.

---

## Six Principles of Professional Conduct

### 1. Responsibilities
- Exercise sensitive professional and moral judgment
- Serve the public interest
- Cooperate with members to improve the profession

### 2. Public Interest
- Act in a way that serves the public interest
- Honor public trust
- Demonstrate commitment to professionalism

### 3. Integrity
- Be honest and candid
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Don't subordinate judgment to others

### 4. Objectivity & Independence
- Be free from conflicts of interest
- Maintain impartiality
- Exercise independence in fact and appearance

### 5. Due Care
- Observe professional and ethical standards
- Continually improve competence
- Discharge professional responsibilities diligently

### 6. Scope & Nature of Services
- Observe principles when determining scope
- Determine services in consultation with client
- Consider if consistent with public responsibilities

## Independence Rules

### Two Types of Independence

| Type | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Independence in Fact | Actual state of mind and objectivity |
| Independence in Appearance | Others' perception of independence |

### Independence Impairments

**Direct Financial Interests:**
- ❌ Owning stock in attest client
- ❌ Investment in client through 401(k)
- ❌ Loans to/from client (with exceptions)

**Family Relationships:**
- Immediate family with key position = impaired
- Close relatives reviewed case-by-case

**Employment Relationships:**
- Former employment: 1-year cooling off
- Key positions at client = impaired

---

## Threats to Independence

### S.A.M.F.U. Mnemonic

| Threat | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **S**elf-interest | Financial interest affecting judgment |
| **A**dvocacy | Promoting client's interests |
| **M**anagement participation | Making management decisions |
| **F**amiliarity | Long association with client |
| **U**ndue influence | Threats from client |

## Safeguards

### Firm-Level Safeguards
- Quality control policies
- Tone at the top
- Partner rotation
- Independent review

### Engagement-Level Safeguards
- Discussion with audit committee
- Review by non-engagement partner
- Documentation of threats and safeguards
- Consultation with ethics hotline

---

## Integrity & Objectivity

### Rule 102 Requirements
- Don't knowingly misrepresent facts
- Don't subordinate judgment
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Disclose conflicts when they exist

### Subordination of Judgment
- Never allowed
- Report up chain of command
- Document disagreements
- Consider resignation if unresolved

---

## Acts Discreditable

### Examples (Rule 501)
- Discrimination or harassment
- Solicitation of clients improperly
- Disclosure of confidential info
- Failures in government audits
- Negligence in tax practice
- Criminal convictions

---

## Confidentiality (Rule 301)

### Information Protected
- All client information
- Work papers
- Communications

### Permissible Disclosures
| Situation | Disclosure Allowed |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Client consent | ✅ Yes |
| Court subpoena | ✅ Yes |
| Peer review | ✅ Yes |
| Ethics investigation | ✅ Yes |
| Suspected fraud (authorities) | ✅ Yes |

---

## Fees & Commissions

### Contingent Fees
- ❌ Not for attest clients
- ✅ OK for non-attest services

### Commissions
- ❌ Not from attest clients
- ✅ OK from non-attest clients if disclosed
- Disclosure required in all cases

---

## Advertising & Solicitation

### Allowed
- ✅ Truthful advertising
- ✅ Yellow page listings
- ✅ Website marketing
- ✅ Testimonials (if true)

### Prohibited
- ❌ False or misleading claims
- ❌ Deceptive practices
- ❌ Coercion
- ❌ Harassment
