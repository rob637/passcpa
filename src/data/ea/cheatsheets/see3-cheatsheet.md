# EA SEE Part 3: Representation, Practices & Procedures Cheatsheet

## Section Overview
- **Exam Weight**: 100 questions (85 scored, 15 pretest)
- **Time**: 3.5 hours (210 minutes)
- **Passing Score**: 105 scaled (approximately 70%)
- **Focus**: Ethics, Circular 230, IRS procedures, representation

---

## Circular 230 Overview

### Who is Covered
- Attorneys
- CPAs
- Enrolled Agents
- Enrolled Actuaries
- Enrolled Retirement Plan Agents
- Registered Tax Return Preparers (limited)

### Practice Before the IRS Includes
- Representing taxpayers at conferences
- Preparing and filing documents
- Correspondence with IRS
- Providing written advice

---

## Duties & Restrictions

### Due Diligence (§10.22)
- Cannot endorse client's position blindly
- Must make reasonable inquiries
- Cannot ignore implications of information

### Competence (§10.35)
- Must have necessary knowledge and skill
- Can become competent through study
- Must know when to refer to others

### Contingent Fees (§10.27)
| Allowed | Prohibited |
|---------|------------|
| Examination or challenge of original return | Preparing original return |
| Amended returns for refund | Preparing refund claim predictably denied |
| IRS claims/audits | |
| Judicial proceedings | |

### Knowledge of Client Errors (§10.21)
- Must advise client promptly of error/omission
- Must advise client of consequences
- Cannot inform IRS without client consent (generally)

### Conflicting Interests (§10.29)
- May represent with informed written consent
- Must reasonably believe competent representation possible
- Cannot if prohibited by law

---

## Return Preparation Standards

### Positions on Returns (§10.34)
- **Realistic possibility standard**: > 33% likelihood of success
- **Substantial authority**: ~40% likelihood for undisclosed positions
- **Reasonable basis + disclosure**: At minimum, not frivolous

### Signing Returns
- Practitioner must sign returns they prepare
- Must furnish copy to taxpayer
- PTIN required for paid preparers

### Reliance on Others
- Can rely on information from clients unless unreasonable
- Cannot ignore obvious errors
- Must make reasonable inquiries

---

## Written Advice (§10.37)

### Requirements
- Must be based on reasonable factual and legal assumptions
- Must consider all relevant facts
- Cannot take unreasonable positions
- Must advise of reliance limits

### Covered Opinions (Historical)
- Marketed tax shelters require specific disclosures
- Heightened standards for tax shelter opinions

---

## Best Practices (§10.33)

### Not Mandatory, But Recommended
1. Communicate clearly with clients
2. Establish facts and evaluate accuracy
3. Advise client of positions and penalties
4. Act fairly and with integrity

---

## Disciplinary Proceedings

### Disreputable Conduct (§10.51)
- Conviction of tax-related crime
- Giving false information to Treasury
- Willfully failing to file returns
- Misappropriating client funds
- Contemptuous conduct
- False or misleading advertising
- Preparing false returns

### Sanctions Available
| Sanction | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Censure | Public reprimand |
| Suspension | Temporary ban on practice |
| Disbarment | Permanent ban on practice |
| Monetary Penalty | Up to $1,000 per violation (indexed) |

### Due Process Rights
- 30-day response to complaint
- Right to hearing before ALJ
- Appeal to OPR Director
- Appeal to Secretary of Treasury

## IRS Examination Process

### Types of Audits

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Correspondence | Letter requesting documents |
| Office Audit | Interview at IRS office |
| Field Audit | Agent visits taxpayer location |
| TCMP Audit | Random compliance audit |

### Statute of Limitations (Assessment)
| Situation | Period |
|-----------|--------|
| Normal | 3 years from filing |
| > 25% gross income omission | 6 years |
| Fraud or no return | Unlimited |
| Form 1099 discrepancy | 6 years |

### Statute of Limitations (Collection)
- 10 years from assessment
- Can be extended by agreement

## Penalties

### Taxpayer Penalties

| Penalty | Rate |
|---------|------|
| Failure to file | 5%/month (max 25%) |
| Failure to pay | 0.5%/month (max 25%) |
| Combined max | 5%/month FTF + 0.5% FTP up to 5 months |
| Accuracy-related | 20% of underpayment |
| Fraud | 75% of underpayment |
| Frivolous return | $5,000 |
| Negligence | 20% |

### Preparer Penalties

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Unreasonable position | Greater of $1,000 or 50% of income |
| Willful/reckless | Greater of $5,000 or 75% of income |
| Failure to sign | $60 per return |
| Failure to furnish copy | $60 per return |
| Failure to retain copy | $60 per return |
| Failure to furnish PTIN | $60 per return |
| EIC due diligence failure | $635 per return |
| CTC/AOTC due diligence | $635 per return |
| HOH due diligence | $635 per return |

## Collection Process

### Collection Stages
1. CP14 Notice (balance due)
2. CP501, CP503, CP504 (increasing urgency)
3. Final Notice of Intent to Levy (30 days)
4. Notice of Federal Tax Lien
5. Levy on assets

### Collection Alternatives

| Option | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Installment Agreement | Monthly payments |
| OIC (Offer in Compromise) | Settle for less than owed |
| CNC (Currently Not Collectible) | Temporary delay |
| Innocent Spouse | Relief from joint liability |
| Injured Spouse | Protect refund from spouse's debt |

### Offer in Compromise Grounds
1. **Doubt as to Liability**: Dispute the tax owed
2. **Doubt as to Collectibility**: Can't pay full amount
3. **Effective Tax Administration**: Hardship or equity

## Taxpayer Rights

### Taxpayer Bill of Rights
1. Right to be informed
2. Right to quality service
3. Right to pay no more than correct amount
4. Right to challenge IRS position
5. Right to appeal in independent forum
6. Right to finality
7. Right to privacy
8. Right to confidentiality
9. Right to retain representation
10. Right to fair and just tax system

### Taxpayer Advocate Service
- Independent IRS organization
- Helps resolve problems not resolved through normal channels
- Filing Form 911

---

## Appeals Process

### IRS Appeals Office
- Alternative dispute resolution
- Fast Track Settlement
- Post-Appeals Mediation
- Can negotiate settlements

### Tax Court
- File petition within 90 days of notice of deficiency
- No prepayment required
- May use Small Tax Case procedure (< $50,000)

### Other Courts
- District Court (prepayment required)
- Court of Federal Claims (prepayment required)
- Court of Appeals (for Tax Court or District Court appeals)

---

## Power of Attorney

### Form 2848
- Authorizes representative to practice before IRS
- Must be signed by taxpayer
- Specifies tax matters and years
- Can be filed electronically via CAF

### Form 8821
- Tax Information Authorization only
- No representation authority
- Just allows receiving copies

### Centralized Authorization File (CAF)
- Electronic POA database
- CAF number assigned to practitioners

## Common Exam Traps

1. **Contingent fees**: Allowed for audits, NOT for original returns
2. **Conflict of interest**: Need written consent, not just verbal
3. **Client errors**: Advise client, but cannot inform IRS without consent
4. **90-day letter**: Must file Tax Court petition within 90 days
5. **Statute for fraud**: Unlimited - no statute runs
6. **Injured vs Innocent Spouse**: Injured protects refund; Innocent relieves liability
7. **OIC**: Cannot be in open bankruptcy
8. **Due diligence forms**: Preparer must complete for EITC, CTC, AOTC, HOH

---

## Mnemonics

- **CIRACLE 230**: Circular 230 rules for practitioners
- **FTF > FTP**: Failure to file penalty > failure to pay penalty
- **3-6-Unlimited**: Assessment periods (normal, 25% omission, fraud)
- **90 Days to Court**: Tax Court petition deadline after notice of deficiency
- **DISCO**: Disreputable conduct triggers (Dishonest, Incomplete, Shady, Criminal, Obstruct)
